Geometric Atlas Visualiser



This application provides an interface to project and visualise stereotaxic coordinates over different representations of a cortical surface reconstruction, and over the geometric model of the cortical surface (Toro, 2003; Toro and Burnod, 2003). Here we describe the interface of the Geometric Atlas Visualiser and the projection of stereotaxic coordinates.

1. Distribution contents and Starting-up

Two items are contained in the Geometric Atlas Visualiser folder:

Geometric Atlas Geometric Atlas Visualiser application
GA_folder Resources folder containing representations of a cortical surface reconstruction (original, smoothed, spherical), mappings of the folding patterns (sulcal depth, integrated mean curvature), anatomy constrained angular coordinates and geometric model textures (folding pattern, folding pattern with sulcal nomenclature, folding pattern with cytoarchitectonic regions)

Geometric Atlas Visualiser is started by double clicking its icon. The Geometric Atlas Visualiser opens two windows, the Panel window and an Untitled document window. New document windows can be created by selecting New from the File menu (command-N). By default the document window presents an original cortical surface reconstruction with sulcal depth mapping and the geometric model overlaid.

2. Interface

2.1 Panel window

The Panel window controls the rendering and projection of coordinates. The Panel window contains three tabs: Render, Talairach and Geometric Atlas.

2.1.1 Render tab



The Render tab controls the rendering of the reconstructed cortical surface representations. The sliders XY, YZ and ZX control the rotation of the surface models in their respective planes. Standard rotations can be obtained by clicking in the "unfolded cube" buttons (S=Superior, A=Anterior, L=Lateral, P=Posterior, M=Medial, I=Inferior). The size of the surface model can be adjusted by the Zoom slider.

Three different representations of the cortical surface reconstruction can be used: Original, Smoothed and Spherical. For each of these representations, a polar stereographic projection can be obtained by clicking the Stereographic button (click the radio buttons to recover the cartesian projections)

Two mappings of the cortical folding patterns can be used: Depth (sulcal depth) and Curvature (integrated mean curvature). Further, the geometric model can be overlaid to these mappings by selecting the Geometric Model checkbox.

2.1.2 Talairach Tab



The Talairach tab controls the projection of stereotaxic coortinates over the representations of the cortical surface reconstruction. Coordinates can be entered by directly typing them in the text field, or by opening coordinates in an external file (Open button). Coordinates can be saved by clicking the Save button.

Important: Coordinate format.
Coordinates are entered in the order Sagital Coronal Axial separated by a space. The "#" symbol can be used to write comments that are not read by the parser.

Example:

# visual  task coordinates
10 -50 0
9 -60 5

The stereotaxic Talairach coordinates are projected over the surface reconstructions by clicking the Project button. The projection is performed by defining a small sphere centered at the three dimensional point defined by the Talairach coordinates. The radius of this sphere can be controlled by the distance text field and slider. The sphere is divided in three concentric spheres. The points over the surface inside the internal concentric sphere are drawn in red, the points in the intermediate sphere are drawn in green and the points in the external concentric sphere are drawn in blue.

Important: Projection of coordinates
A single coordinate may produce multiple projections over the cortical surface. For example, a point inside a sulcus will produce projections at both sulcal walls.

The projection of stereotaxic coordinates can be visualised in all the surface representations and their associated stereographic projections. Changing or reselecting a mapping of the folding patterns erases the stereotaxic coordinates projection, which can be recovered by clicking the Project button again.

2.1.3 Geometric Atlas tab



The Geometric Atlas tab allows to visualise the stereotaxic coordinates over the geometric model of the cortical surface. Resising the Panel window resizes also the Geometric Model image. This image can be saved (in TIFF format) by clicking the Save Image button.

Note: Circular buttons
The circular buttons control three preliminary functionalities.

1. The left-most button allows to project the data projected over the surface reconstruction (folding pattern mappings, for example) in the geometric model.

2. The middel button allows to change the geometric model image.

3. The right-most button generates a QuickTime movie from the rotation of the surface reconstruction around the YZ axis.

2.2 Document window

The document window contains the actual cortical surface reconstruction rendering. The document window contains two tabs: Cortical Surface and Geometric Atlas

2.2.1 Cortical Surface tab



The Cortical Surface tab present the selected cortical surface reconstruction, which can be interactively rotated by clicking over the model. Resizing the document window resizes the surface model. The model image can be saved (as a TIFF file) by clicking the Save Image button.

2.2.2 Geometric Atlas tab



The Geometric Atlas tab is under construction. At present it displays the geometric model with sulcal nomenclature overlaid. In a future update it will also display the projected stereotaxic coordinates, as it done in the Geometric Atlas tab in the Panel window. Resizing the document window resizes the geometric model. The geometric model image can be saved (as a TIFF file) by clicking the Save Image button.

3. Projection of stereotaxic coordinates: a quick tutorial

  1. Double click the Geometric Atlas icon. The Geometric Atlas Visualiser Starts-up.
  2. Select the Talairach tab in the Panel window.
  3. Copy (command-C) and Paste (command-V) the following text

    #view colour
    22 -73 -8
    20 -66 -4
    26 -68 -8
    24 -70 -5

    #optical flow
    28 -80 -8
    40 -66 -8
    22 -86 4
    22  -86 8
    22 -76 36
    38 -60 -8

  4. Select a distance of 5mm by typing it in the Distance text field or by acting over the slider.
  5. Click the Project button to project the coordinates
  6. In the document window, select the Cortical Surface tab and rotate the surface model to expose its medial surface where the coortinates are mapped.
  7. You can create a text file with the coortinates you entered by clicking the Save button.
  8. The figures show these coordinates mapped over the original cortical surface reconstruction, a smoothed representation and a stereographic representation.
